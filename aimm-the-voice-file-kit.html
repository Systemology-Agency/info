The Voice File Kit.

Two copy paste prompts that teach AI to write like you. Build the file once, use it everywhere.

Every AI writes in the same polished, generic voice by default. This kit builds one small file that teaches it your voice instead. You talk, it listens, and you keep the file for good.

How it works

1

Paste Prompt 1

The AI interviews you. It asks 100 questions total, sent in small batches, and waits for your answers before it sends the next batch. Talk your answers out loud with voice dictation instead of typing them. Talking gets you the real answer. Typing gets you the tidy one.

2

Paste Prompt 2 In The Same Chat

It compresses everything from the interview into one file. That file stays under 5,000 tokens, which is roughly a few pages of text, so it is easy to carry around and easy for AI to read fast.

3

Save It And Load It Anywhere

Save the file. Load it wherever your AI writes for you, in Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini, before you ask it to write anything.

Prompt 1: The Interview

Copy this. Paste it into a new chat with your AI.

I want you to learn how I actually write and talk, so you can write in my voice from now on. Interview me. Ask me 100 questions total, in batches of 5, and wait for my answers before you send the next batch. Cover: the words and phrases I use a lot, the words I would never use, how I greet people and how I sign off, my opinions and hot takes about my work, the jokes I make, how casual I am with different kinds of people, how long my sentences usually run, and how I sound in a text versus an email. Along the way, ask me to paste in three emails or texts I wrote myself, and study them. If I give you a short or polite answer, ask a follow-up until you get the real one. I will answer by talking with voice dictation, so expect rambling. The rambling is the good stuff. Do not summarize as we go. Just collect.

Prompt 2: The Compressor

Paste this in the same chat, right after the interview is done.

Now turn everything you learned in this interview into one file I can hand to any AI so it writes like me. Keep it under 5,000 tokens. Include: a short description of my overall voice; the words and phrases I love, with examples; the words and phrases I never use; how I open and close messages; my go-to opinions; the kind of humor I use and when; three to five real examples of my writing, copied word for word from what I pasted; and a short set of rules for what to do when you are not sure, with this default: shorter, plainer, more direct. Cut anything generic that could describe anyone. Title the file 'My Voice File'. Output only the file and nothing else.

Where to put the file

Claude: add it to a Project's knowledge, or the folder Claude reads at the start of a session.

ChatGPT: add it to a Project, or paste the heart of it into custom instructions.

Gemini: add it to a Gem's instructions.

When the way you write changes, edit this one file. Everything you make with AI follows.
